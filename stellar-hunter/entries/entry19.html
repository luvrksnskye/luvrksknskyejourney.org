You have captured Arcturus! The name Arcturus comes from the ancient Greek word "Arktouros," meaning "bear guardian," as it appears to follow the constellations Ursa Major and Ursa Minor across the sky. Arcturus is located about 36.7 light-years from Earth, making it one of the closest bright stars to us. It is also easy to locate by following the arc of Ursa Major's tail (the Chariot asterism).

In Greek mythology, Arcturus is the guardian of the bears, associated with the protection of the constellations Ursa Major and Ursa Minor. The ancient Polynesians also used it as a guiding star for navigation.
