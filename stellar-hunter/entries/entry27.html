The Morning Herald



The constellation Dawn appears just before dawn, its stars forming the shape of a cat in the middle of its stretch. According to legend, it was the companion of the Sun god, who woke him up every morning by tapping his nose with her paw. Without her, the sun would sleep and the world would remain in darkness. Her constellation is most visible in the hours before dawn, and it is said that early risers who see it will have a successful day.
